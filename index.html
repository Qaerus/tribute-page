A Tribute to Ada Lovelace

About Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace (1815–1852) was an English mathematician and writer, chiefly known for her work on Charles Babbage's early mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine. She is often regarded as the first computer programmer because she created an algorithm intended to be processed by a machine.

Lovelace's notes contain what is recognized as the first algorithm intended to be carried out by a machine, making her a pioneering figure in computer science.

Famous Quote

"That brain of mine is something more than merely mortal; as time will show."
